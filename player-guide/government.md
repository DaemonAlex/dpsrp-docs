# Government System Guide

Understanding city government on DPSRP.

## Overview

DPSRP uses **ap-government** for a full city government system including the Mayor, City Council, elections, and taxation.

## Government Structure

### Mayor
The elected leader of Los Santos:
- Sets city policies
- Controls tax rates
- Manages city budget
- Appoints officials
- Signs legislation

### City Council
Legislative body that:
- Proposes laws
- Votes on ordinances
- Advises Mayor
- Represents districts

## Elections

### Running for Office
1. Register as a candidate
2. Campaign to voters
3. Participate in debates
4. Election day voting
5. Results announced

### Voting
All citizens can vote in elections:
- Register to vote
- Go to polling location
- Cast your ballot
- One vote per person

### Election Cycles
- Mayor: Regular election cycles
- Council: Staggered terms
- Special elections as needed

## Taxation

### Tax Types
The city collects various taxes:

| Tax Type | Rate | Applied To |
|----------|------|------------|
| Income Tax | 15% | Job earnings |
| Item Tax | 70% | Purchases |
| Vehicle Tax | 20% | Vehicle purchases |
| Business Tax | 15% | Business income |
| Housing Tax | 20% | Property purchases |

*Rates subject to change by Mayor*

### Mayor Tax Control
The Mayor can adjust tax rates:
- Raise or lower taxes
- Fund city services
- Balance the budget
- Respond to economic needs

### Where Taxes Go
- City services
- Infrastructure
- Emergency services
- Public programs
