# Documents & Paperwork

*"Sign here, initial there, and don't forget the notary stamp!"*

Welcome to the wonderful world of official documents. From business licenses to restraining orders, if it needs paper, we've got you covered.

## Getting Documents

### Where to Start

Need official paperwork? Head to **City Hall** and find:
- The reception desk for standard forms
- The Mayor's office for business documents
- The Bar Association for legal forms

### Types of Documents

**Personal Documents:**
- ID Cards
- Licenses
- Permits
- Certifications

**Business Documents:**
- Registration papers
- Tax forms
- Grant applications
- Contracts

**Legal Documents:**
- Court summons
- Subpoenas
- Legal motions
- Evidence records

## Creating Documents

### Blank Documents

Need to draft something official? Get blank documents from City Hall. These can be customized for:
- Contracts between parties
- Agreements and deals
- Official notices
- Custom forms

### Making Copies

Got a **Portable Document Copier**? You can:
- Duplicate important papers
- Create backups of vital docs
- Share copies with others
- Keep records for yourself

## Using Documents

### Presenting Your Papers

When asked for documents:
1. Open your inventory
2. Select the document
3. Show it to the requesting party
4. They can verify authenticity

### Document Security

- Keep important documents safe
- Don't leave them lying around
- Some documents can be forged (but that's illegal!)
- Authorities can verify authenticity

## Tips

1. **Always keep copies** - You never know when you'll need backup
2. **Organize your papers** - A messy inventory means lost documents
3. **Know what you need** - Different situations require different forms
4. **Keep it official** - Forged documents have serious consequences
